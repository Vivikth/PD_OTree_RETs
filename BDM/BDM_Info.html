In this experiment, I want to determine how much you value objects or tasks. I will use the switch-point procedure for this purpose. I'll explain the procedure using 2 examples.


Example One

Let's suppose that I want to determine how much you value a pencil, in terms of money. I would ask you the following questions:

In each question, you pick either option A (the pencil) or option B (the money). After you answer all 2001 questions, I will randomly pick one question, and give you the option you chose on that question. Each question is equally likely to be chosen for payment. Obviously you have no incentive to lie on any question, because if that question gets chosen for payment then you’d end up with the option you like less.

I assume you’re going to choose the Option A in at least the first few questions, but at some point switch to choosing Option B. So, to save time, just tell me at which dollar value you’d switch. I can then ‘fill out’ your answers to all 2001 questions based on your switch point (choosing Option A for all questions before your switch point, and Option B for all questions at or after your switch point).

I’ll still draw one question randomly for payment. Again, if you lie about your true switch point you might end up getting an option that you like less.

Like the monetary example above, in each question, you pick either option A (the pencil) or option B (the lottery).
However in this case, there will be a 5% chance that I randomly pick a question, and give you the option you chose on that question.
You still have no incentive to lie on any question, because if that question gets chosen for payment then you’d end up with the option you like less.

If you chose the pencil on the question I picked, you would get the pencil for sure.
If you chose the lottery on the question I picked, I would run the lottery with the probabilities specified in the question I picked.
You’ll get to leave the experiment (and receive the full monetary compensation) if that is the outcome from the lottery, or have to watch the boring video and answer questions otherwise.

Again, if you lie about your true switch point you might end up getting an option that you like less.
